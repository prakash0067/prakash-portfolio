An Analysis of Handover Techniques, Management, and Challenges in Edge Computing at Multiple Access Points

This paper explores the critical role of handover techniques in Multi-Access Edge Computing (MEC), which operates at the network edge to deliver low-latency services to end users. A primary focus is on how handoff strategies enable the seamless migration of device and application data between MEC nodes, a fundamental requirement for supporting device mobility. The research also reviews the challenges and methods associated with MEC handover, including resource allocation at the target node and the migration of application contexts. This work highlights the importance of combining handover techniques with MEC node selection to ensure smooth transitions and maintain a high quality of experience for users.
